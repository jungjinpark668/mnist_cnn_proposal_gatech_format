# References

> **Guideline Reference:** References should be presented in a consistent manner throughout the proposal. It is recommended that references be presented in either **consecutive number order** or **alphabetical by first author** of the publication.

---

## Reference Formatting Guidelines

### Option A: Consecutive Numbering

When using consecutive numbers, the list of references is prepared in numerical order following the first appearance of each reference in the text.

**In-text citation style:** `[1]`, `[2]`, `[3]`, ...

**Reference list example:**

> [1] A. Smith, B. Jones, and C. Lee, "Title of the paper," *Journal Name*, vol. 10, no. 3, pp. 100-110, 2023.
>
> [2] D. Wang and E. Chen, "Another paper title," in *Proc. Conference Name*, City, Country, 2022, pp. 50-55.
>
> [3] F. Garcia, *Book Title*. Publisher City: Publisher Name, 2021.

### Option B: Alphabetical by First Author

When using alphabetical listing, the references in the body of the proposal should give the author's last name and the year of the publication.

**In-text citation style:**
- One author: `(Smith, 2023)`
- Two authors: `(Smith and Jones, 2023)` — list both names
- Three or more authors: `(Smith *et al.*, 2023)` — first author + *et al.*

**Reference list example:**

> Garcia, F. (2021). *Book Title*. Publisher City: Publisher Name.
>
> Smith, A., Jones, B., and Lee, C. (2023). "Title of the paper." *Journal Name*, 10(3), 100-110.
>
> Wang, D. and Chen, E. (2022). "Another paper title." In *Proc. Conference Name*, City, Country, pp. 50-55.
